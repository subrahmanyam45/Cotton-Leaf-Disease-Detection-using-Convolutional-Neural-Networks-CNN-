Ascochyta leaf spot is characterized by the development of small, circular lesions on cotyledons and leaves of cotton plants. These lesions typically have a reddish-brown to dark brown color and may exhibit a yellowish halo around them. The disease is favored by warm and humid conditions. It can spread through various means, including infected seeds, plant debris, and wind-driven rain. Management strategies for Ascochyta Leaf Spot involve a combination of practices. Crop rotation is recommended to reduce the prevalence of the disease, as well as the planting of disease-resistant cotton varieties. Additionally, timely application of fungicides can be effective in controlling the spread of the disease.
